Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose
-the man who fought for India.

Here is a timeline of Netaji's life.

  • 1897- Born at Cuttack, Orissa Division.
  • 1902- Admitted to Protestant European School(presently Stewart High School) in Cuttack.
  • 1909- Shifted to Ravenshaw Collegiate School.
  • 1913- Secured second position in the matriculation examination and got admission to the Presidency College.
  • 1918- Passed his B.A. in philosophy from Calcutta University.
  • 1919- Came fourth in the ICS examination and was selected, but he did not want to work under an alien government which would mean serving the British.
  • 1921- Resigned from civil service job and wrote to his elder brother Sarat Chandra Bose : "Only on the soil of sacrifice and suffering can we raise our national edifice."
  • 1923- Elected as the President of All India Youth Congress and also the Secretary fo Bengal State Congress.
  • 1924- Elected as the mayor of Calcutta Municipal Corporation.
  • 1925- Arrested and was sent to prison in Mandalay, where he contracted tuberculosis.
  • 1927- Become General Secretary of the Congress party and worked with Jawaharlal Nehru for independence.
  • 1928- Organized the Annual Meeting of the Indian National Congress in Calcutta.
  • 1930- Travelled in Europe, visiting Indian students and European politicians, including Benito Mussolini .
  • 1938- Had become the leader of national stature and agreed to accept nomination as Congress president.
  • 1939- Organized the All India forward Bloc on June 22 a faction within the Indian National Congress, aimed at consolidating the political left, but its main strength was in his home state, Bengal.
  • 1941- Escaped from under British surveillance from his Elign road house in Calcutta about 01:25AM on 17 January of 1941, accompanied by his nephew Sisir Kumar Bose in a German made Wandered W24 Sedan car and went to Russia.
  • 1943- Formed Azad Hind Fauj on
  • 1943- Rash Bihari Bose handed the control of Indian National Army (INA) to Subhas_Chandra_Bose.
  • 1944- The famous quote said by Subhas : "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" He also urged the people of India to join him in his fight against the British Raj.
  • 1944- Addressed Mahatma Gandhi as "Father of the Nation" and asked his blessings and good wishes for the war he was fighting.
  • 1945- Died on 18th August after his overloaded Japanese plane crashed.

    "Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose in order to free India from the shackles of British imperialism organized the Azad Hind Government from outside the country on October 21, 1943. Netaji set up the Provisional Government of Independent India (Azad Hind) and transferred its headquarter at Rangoon on January 7, 1944. On the 5th April, 1944, the "Azad Hind Bank" was inaugurated at Rangoon. It was on this occasion that Netaji used this chair for the first time. Later the chair was kept at the residence of Netaji at 51, University Avenue, Rangoon, where the office of the Azad Hind Government was also housed. Afterwards,at the time of leaving Burma, the Britishers handed over the chair to the family of Mr.A.T.Ahuja, the well known business man of Rangoon. The chair was officially handed over to the Government of India in January 1979. It was brought to Calcutta on the 17th July, 1980. It has now been ceremonially installed at the Red Fort on July 7, 1981."
